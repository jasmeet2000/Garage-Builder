Attached vs. Detached

Which style is best suited for your property, budget, and local zoning bylaws in Calgary?

Home › Blog › Attached vs. Detached
May 18, 2026 PeakCity Crew Design Options

If you are planning to build a new garage in Calgary, one of your primary design choices will be whether to build an attached structure connected directly to your house, or a standalone detached garage in the backyard.

In many of Calgary's master-planned communities—like Mahogany, Evanston, or Silverado—the decision is dictated by the lot layout (many homes are built with rear lane access specifically designed for detached back alley garages). However, for properties where both options are physically possible, making the right choice requires looking at budget, construction bylaws, safety, and property value. Let's compare both options in detail.

1. The Financial Comparison (Cost to Build)

Many homeowners assume that building an attached garage is cheaper because they "already have one wall built" (the side of the house). In reality, the opposite is true.

  • Attached Garage Costs: Often **20% to 40% more expensive** to construct. This is because connecting the garage structure to an existing home foundation requires careful excavation, tying into the existing structural framing, roofing tie-ins, and complex waterproofing. Furthermore, municipal codes dictate that you must install expensive firewall barriers between the house and garage, along with sealed fire-rated doors.
  • Detached Garage Costs: Standalone builds are **significantly cheaper** and faster. The crew has open access to the site with heavy excavation machinery. The concrete pad can be poured independently, and the framing does not require structural adjustments to your existing home walls.

2. Calgary Lot Restrictions & Bylaws

Your property's layout and zoning are the ultimate deciders. In Calgary:

  • Rear Lane Access: If your home has a back alley, building a detached garage at the rear of the property is highly recommended. It keeps vehicle noise away from the main home living areas and complies perfectly with "accessory residential building" zoning.
  • Lot Coverage Limits: An attached garage counts as part of your main house coverage. A detached garage falls under accessory building coverage (capped at 10% of lot area or 75 square meters). If your home already has a large footprint, a detached garage is often easier to permit without exceeding coverage limits.

Note on fire safety: If an attached garage has a vehicle fire, smoke and fire can quickly spread into the living quarters. A detached garage isolates vehicle hazards, gas fumes, paint cans, and propane tanks away from your sleeping areas.

3. Heating & Insulation in Alberta

With Calgary winters regularly dropping below -25°C, insulation is key:

  • Attached Garages: Benefit slightly from the shared wall of the heated house, maintaining a slightly warmer temperature even without dedicated heaters. However, if the garage is poorly insulated, it can draw heat *away* from the rooms located directly above it.
  • Detached Garages: Are easier and cleaner to insulate independently. They are perfect for workshop spaces, as you can run a dedicated gas heater, sub-panel, and insulate the walls with R-20 batting without transferring mechanical noise or toxic carbon monoxide exhaust into your home.

4. Architectural & Aesthetic Appeal

How does each impact curb appeal and home value?

  • Attached Garages: Can dominate the front facade of your home (often referred to as a "snout house" if the garage protrudes forward). Matching the siding, roofing angle, and window trims perfectly is critical.
  • Detached Garages: Allow the front of your home to remain open, placing focus on a beautiful front porch and garden. The garage in the back can be styled to match the home trims perfectly, providing a cohesive yard design.

Conclusion: Which Should You Choose?

Choose an **Attached Garage** if you have front-street access only, hate walking outside in Calgary's freezing winter weather to get to your vehicle, and have a larger budget for structural tie-ins.

Choose a **Detached Garage** if you have a back lane lot, want a cheaper and faster build, need a dedicated workshop/hobby space free from noise concerns, or want to explore building a **garage suite (laneway house)** in the future.
