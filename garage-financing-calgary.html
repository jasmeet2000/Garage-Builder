Calgary Garage Financing Options

We work with multiple financing options so you can find the best fit for your budget and credit profile. All options available to Calgary homeowners.

Most Popular

Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

Use the equity already built in your home to finance your garage construction. HELOCs typically offer the lowest interest rates of any financing option and are revolving credit you can draw from as needed.

Lowest rates (Prime + 0.5% typical)
Interest-only payments available
Draw funds as work progresses
Tax advantages in some situations
Available through any Canadian bank or credit union

Personal Loan / Unsecured Loan

A personal loan from your bank or credit union offers a fixed interest rate and predictable monthly payments — ideal if you prefer a clear repayment schedule with a defined end date.

Fixed rate, fixed monthly payment
No home equity required
Loan terms 3–10 years
Fast approval (often same-day)
Good for amounts up to $75,000

Mortgage Refinancing

Refinance your existing mortgage to pull out equity for construction. This can be the most cost-effective option for large projects as mortgage rates are typically lower than personal loan rates.

Access large amounts ($50K–$200K+)
Very low interest rates
Long amortization reduces monthly payments
Can bundle into existing mortgage
Best for large or luxury builds

Contractor Payment Plans

PeakCity Garages offers flexible milestone-based payment schedules. Pay in stages as your garage progresses — typically 10% deposit, 40% on foundation, 40% on framing, 10% on completion.

No credit check required
Spreads payments across build timeline
Pay only as work is completed
Protects you at every stage
Standard industry practice

If your garage includes energy-efficient features (insulation, heat pump, EV charging), you may qualify for federal grants through the Canada Greener Homes initiative. Our team can advise on eligible upgrades.

Construction Loan

Specifically designed for building projects, a construction loan releases funds in stages as construction milestones are met. Once your garage is complete, it typically converts to a conventional mortgage or loan.

Purpose-built for construction
Progress-draw structure
Interest-only during build phase
Converts to term loan on completion
Works well for large projects

Garage Financing FAQs

Can I finance a garage build in Calgary without a down payment?

Yes, through a HELOC you can typically access funds with no down payment, using your existing home equity as collateral. Many Calgary homeowners with 20%+ equity in their home can access $50,000–$100,000 this way. Your bank or mortgage broker can advise on your specific situation.

What credit score do I need to finance a garage?

For a personal loan, most Canadian lenders prefer a credit score of 660+. For a HELOC or mortgage refinance, you'll typically need 680+ and sufficient home equity. Even with less-than-perfect credit, options exist — private lenders or credit unions may be more flexible. Contact us and we'll help connect you with the right resource.

Does PeakCity Garages offer in-house financing?

Currently, PeakCity Garages does not offer in-house financing directly. However, we work with our clients' lenders and structure payment schedules to align with construction draws, making the process seamless. We also connect clients with trusted mortgage brokers who specialize in construction financing.

Can a garage suite income help pay my mortgage?

Absolutely. A legal garage suite in Calgary can rent for $1,200–$2,200/month. At these rates, a $95,000 garage suite can be fully financed and paid off in 4–6 years using rental income. Many Calgary homeowners use this strategy to effectively build a free garage and generate long-term passive income.

How long does it take to get financing approved for a garage build?

A HELOC application through your existing bank can be approved in 2–5 business days if you have equity available. A personal loan can be approved same-day to 3 business days. Mortgage refinancing takes 2–6 weeks. We recommend beginning the financing conversation before you're ready to start, so funds are available when permits are approved.
