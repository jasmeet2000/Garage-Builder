Calgary Garage Permit Rules

Do you need a permit to build a detached garage in Calgary? Here's the complete guide to city bylaws, setbacks, and building codes.

Home › Blog › Permits
June 1, 2026 PeakCity Crew Permits & Regulations

Building a new detached garage is one of the best ways to add storage, protect your vehicles from heavy Alberta snow, and increase your Calgary property value. However, before you start digging foundations, you must understand the City of Calgary's zoning, land bylaws, and permit regulations.

Failure to comply with these rules can result in costly project delays, heavy municipal fines, and even a court order to demolish the completed structure. In this comprehensive guide, we explain everything you need to know about getting your garage project approved seamlessly.

Development Permit vs. Building Permit: What's the Difference?

Many homeowners confuse these two permits. They represent distinct municipal approvals:

  • Development Permit (DP): Evaluates the impact of your building on the surrounding neighborhood. It checks land use bylaws, lot coverage, and architectural guidelines. You do not always need a DP for a standard residential garage, provided you meet all standard rules.
  • Building Permit (BP): Reviews the technical safety aspects of the build. It ensures the concrete pad, structural wood framing, roof rafters, and electrical installations meet the Alberta Building Code. A Building Permit is always required for any new garage in Calgary.

When Do You Need a Development Permit in Calgary?

For most Calgary neighborhoods under R-1, R-C1, or R-2 zoning, a detached garage is considered an "accessory residential building." You will only need a Development Permit if your proposed garage violates any of the following parameters:

1. Property Setbacks (Property Lines)

Setbacks are the legal distances required between your garage and the property boundaries. Standard Calgary setbacks are:

  • Rear Property Line: Must be at least 0.6 meters (2.0 feet) from the rear line. If the vehicle door faces the rear lane (alley), it must be at least 0.6m or more than 1.5m, though we strongly recommend a minimum of 1.5m to allow a safe vehicle turning radius.
  • Side Property Line: Must be at least 0.6 meters (2.0 feet) from the side boundaries. If the garage is built on a corner lot adjacent to a street, a larger setback is required.
  • Main House Separator: A detached garage must be located at least 1.0 meter (3.3 feet) away from your home or any other accessory building.

2. Lot Coverage

Lot coverage is the maximum percentage of your land area that can be covered by buildings (house, deck, shed, and garage combined). In Calgary, the total coverage of all accessory buildings (including your garage) on a residential lot cannot exceed 10% of the total lot area, or 75 square meters (807 sq. ft.), whichever is smaller.

3. Height Limits

To avoid blocking light and views for neighbors, Calgary dictates strict height limits. For a standard detached garage, the maximum height is 4.6 meters (15.0 feet). This is measured from the ground level directly below to the highest peak of the roofline.

Standard Code Requirements for Calgary Garages

When our crews build your garage, we ensure all components adhere perfectly to the Alberta Building Code. Key specifications include:

  1. Concrete Footing & Pad: Standard garages require an engineered concrete slab with steel reinforcement bars. Thickened edges (minimum 8-inch depth) prevent shifting due to frost heave.
  2. Fire Separation: If your garage is built closer than 1.2 meters (3.9 feet) to your house or property lines, special fire-resistant materials (like 5/8" Type X drywall) are required on the interior wall adjacent to that boundary.
  3. Electrical Outlets: Calgary code requires at least one dedicated circuit for the garage door opener, one interior outlet, and one exterior GFCI-protected outlet. If you are charging an EV, we install a dedicated sub-panel and 240V plug.
