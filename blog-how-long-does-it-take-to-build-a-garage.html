Garage Building Timeline

From breaking ground to the final handover. Understand exactly how long it takes to build a detached garage in Calgary.

Home › Blog › Timeline
May 25, 2026 PeakCity Crew Construction Steps

When you decide to invest in a custom garage, one of your very first questions will likely be: "How long before I can park my car inside?"

In Calgary, the actual on-site construction of a standard 2-car detached garage takes only **2 to 3 weeks**. However, the total project timeline—from your first phone call to driving over the concrete threshold—is usually **6 to 10 weeks**. This is because the design, architectural drawings, property survey checks, and City of Calgary permit approvals represent a large portion of the timeline. Let's break down each phase so you know exactly what to expect.

Phase 1: Planning, Layout, and Permits (4 to 8 Weeks)

This is the most critical phase, though no work is happening in your backyard yet. We do all the heavy lifting behind the scenes to secure municipal approval:

  1. Initial Site Review (1-3 Days): We visit your property, check boundaries, check slope conditions, review your Real Property Report (RPR), and verify Enmax or Atco utility line locations (Alberta One Call click-checks).
  2. Architectural Design (2-5 Days): We design your layout, determine window/door placement, select roof lines (gale vs. hip truss configurations), and match your home's siding.
  3. City of Calgary Permits (4-6 Weeks): We submit building plans to the City. The city's review time varies depending on the season (spring and summer represent peak volume). If your yard requires a Development Permit due to unique lot boundaries, this can add an extra 4 weeks to this phase.

Pro Tip: Want your garage ready for winter? Start your permit process in **June or July**. If you wait until September, permit review delays can push excavation work into freezing winter temperatures, requiring ground heaters that increase costs.

Phase 2: On-Site Construction (2 to 3 Weeks)

Once the city stamps our permits, our crew arrives! Here is the day-to-day schedule of physical construction:

Week 1: Site Prep and Concrete

  • Day 1-2: Excavation & Grading: We remove sod, grade soil to establish correct drainage slopes, lay down compacted gravel fill, and build robust wood forms.
  • Day 3: Steel Rebar Installation: We weave thick steel reinforcement bars in a grid layout to withstand vehicle weight and resist Alberta's expanding clay soils.
  • Day 4: Concrete Pour: We pour a high-strength (32 MPa) concrete mix. Our concrete finishers level, trowel, and cut control joints.
  • Day 5-7: Concrete Curing: Wood forms are removed. The slab must cure for at least 5 days to reach 70% structural strength before we place heavy lumber on it.

Week 2: Framing & Roofing

  • Day 8-9: Wall Framing: We frame the sill plates, wall studs, headers, window openings, and double top plates. The skeletal walls rise quickly!
  • Day 10-11: Truss Installation & Roof Sheathing: Engineered roof trusses are raised, braced, and sheeted with plywood.
  • Day 12-14: Roofing & Siding: We lay ice and water shield, install asphalt shingles (matching your home), and apply house wrap, vinyl/Hardie siding, trim, and gutters.

Week 3: Doors, Electrical & Inspections

  • Day 15: Garage Doors & Windows: Insulated overhead doors, the side entry door, and windows are installed.
  • Day 16-17: Electrical Wiring: Our certified electricians run conduits, mount outlets, install overhead LED panels, set the automatic opener, and wire dedicated lines for sub-panels or EV chargers.
  • Day 18-20: Inspections & Handover: The City of Calgary inspectors visit to review framing, electrical, and final structural compliance. Once approved, we do a thorough yard clean-up, final walkthrough with you, and hand over the keys!

What Factors Can Cause Delays?

While we pride ourselves on building on schedule, external factors can occasionally disrupt the timeline:

  • Severe Weather: Heavy Alberta rains in June or freezing blizzards in winter can delay concrete pouring or paint finishing.
  • Zoning Violations: If your project requires a Development Permit due to tight property borders, the City review will automatically extend the timeline.
  • Utility Easements: If utility lines run under your proposed build site, Enmax or Atco must verify or relocate lines, adding 1 to 2 weeks.
