Backyard Garage Suites

What is a garage suite, what are Calgary's land use rules, and is it a smart investment for your property?

Home › Blog › Garage Suites
April 28, 2026 PeakCity Crew Garage Suites

As Calgary continues to grow, housing density and smart urban planning have become major points of focus. Under recent municipal zoning updates—including the **R-CG and H-GO rezoning initiatives**—building secondary suites has become significantly easier for local property owners.

One of the most exciting secondary suite options is a **Garage Suite** (also referred to as a garden suite, laneway house, or carriage house). This is a self-contained dwelling unit located above a detached backyard garage, featuring its own kitchen, bathroom, sleeping quarters, and separate entry. In this guide, we discuss zoning rules, build costs, utility connections, and financial return on investment (ROI).

1. Calgary's Zoning Rules for Garage Suites

Previously, building a garage suite required a long, expensive zoning amendment process. Today, the City of Calgary has streamlined approvals. A garage suite is considered a **discretionary use** in R-1, R-C1, R-2, and R-CG zones, provided it adheres to standard parameters:

  • Minimum Lot Width: The property lot must be at least 9.0 meters (29.5 feet) wide.
  • Maximum Suite Floor Area: The living space cannot exceed **75 square meters (807 sq. ft.)** or the footprint area of the garage below, whichever is smaller.
  • Maximum Height Limit: Because it is a two-story structure, the height limit is increased to **7.5 meters (24.6 feet)**, measured from ground to peak.
  • Amenity Space: Homeowners must provide at least 30 square meters of outdoor amenity space (like a private backyard patio or balcony) dedicated exclusively to the suite tenant.
  • Parking Requirement: The City usually requires at least one dedicated on-site parking stall for the tenant, which can be located inside the garage or as an outdoor pad adjacent to the alley.

2. Structural and Engineering Challenges

Building a garage suite is vastly different from building a standard garage shell. It is structurally a **two-story house** and requires complex engineering:

  1. Engineered Foundation: A standard concrete pad is insufficient. The foundation must feature deep, structural concrete pilings or grade beams to support the massive load of the second floor.
  2. Utility Connections: The suite requires water, sewer, natural gas, and electrical utilities. In Calgary, utilities must be run underground from the **main house** out to the backyard garage, requiring deep trenching through your yard. Enmax will often require upgrading the home's main panel to 200 Amps.
  3. Fire and Safety Codes: The ceiling of the garage (floor of the suite) must have heavy fire rating (multiple layers of drywall) and be completely sealed to prevent carbon monoxide exhaust fumes from the vehicles below entering the living space.

Estimated Cost: Because they require custom engineering, utility trenching, insulation, plumbing, and interior finish work, a detached garage suite in Calgary generally starts around **$180,000 to $250,000+** depending on size and interior luxury.

3. The Financial Return on Investment (ROI)

Despite the high upfront building costs, garage suites represent outstanding long-term investments:

  • Stable Rental Income: A modern 1-bedroom laneway suite in highly desirable Calgary neighborhoods (like Kensington, Bridgeland, or Altadore) can command **$1,600 to $2,200+ per month** in rental income, paying off the build cost within 8 to 12 years.
  • Property Resale Value: Houses featuring legal, fully-permitted secondary suites are highly sought after by buyers, adding substantial value to your property's resale list price.
  • Multigenerational Living: They are perfect for elderly parents who want independent living close to family, or adult children saving for their own home deposits.
