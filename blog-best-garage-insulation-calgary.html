Garage Insulation Guide

Calgary winters are freezing. Discover the best insulation materials, vapor barriers, and R-value targets to protect your garage.

Home › Blog › Insulation Guide
May 10, 2026 PeakCity Crew Finishing & Insulation

With winter temperatures in Calgary routinely dropping to -30°C, an uninsulated detached garage quickly turns into a freezer. Not only does this make entering your vehicle incredibly uncomfortable, but freezing temperatures also drain your car battery, thicken engine fluids, and limit the usefulness of your space.

Insulating your garage is the ultimate solution. It turns a cold storage shell into a comfortable, multi-use space that can serve as a year-round workshop, gym, or hobby room. In this guide, we discuss the best insulation materials, R-value targets, vapor barrier codes, and heating setups for Calgary properties.

1. Understanding R-Values: What's the Target?

An "R-value" measures a material's thermal resistance to heat flow. The higher the R-value, the better the insulating performance. In Calgary, to keep your heating bills reasonable and maintain a comfortable space, we target the following values:

  • Exterior Walls (2x6 Framing): Target **R-20** insulation. We use thick batting that fits perfectly between standard 16-inch or 24-inch stud cavities.
  • Garage Ceiling / Attic: Target **R-32 to R-40**. Because hot air rises, insulating the roof space is critical. We recommend blown-in cellulose or fiberglass insulation in the attic rafters.
  • Garage Door: Target an insulated overhead door with a minimum **R-12** rating. A cheap uninsulated steel door will dump heat instantly, undermining all wall insulation.

2. Top Garage Insulation Materials Compared

Several materials are available, each with unique pros and cons:

A. Fiberglass Batt Insulation (Most Popular)

Fiberglass batts are the standard for most garage builds. They are cheap, easy to install between studs, and offer solid thermal protection.

  • R-Value: Approx R-3.0 to R-3.7 per inch.
  • Verdict: Ideal for standard double garages on a budget. Must be paired with a poly vapor barrier to prevent moisture damage.

B. Spray Foam Insulation (Premium Performance)

Liquid polyurethane is sprayed directly into stud cavities. It expands rapidly to fill every crack, providing an absolute airtight seal and eliminating all draft points.

  • R-Value: Approx R-6.0 to R-7.0 per inch (closed-cell).
  • Verdict: The absolute best option if you plan to heat your garage 24/7 or are building a **garage suite** above. It is more expensive but yields the highest long-term energy savings.

C. Mineral Wool (Rockwool) Batts

Made from volcanic rock, Rockwool is a dense batt insulation that is highly fire-resistant, water-repellent, and offers superior soundproofing properties.

  • R-Value: Approx R-4.0 per inch.
  • Verdict: Perfect if you are using your garage as a loud workshop or music studio and want to prevent noise from disturbing neighbors.

Crucial Code Step: The Vapor Barrier. Under the Alberta Building Code, any insulated heated wall must have a **6-mil poly vapor barrier** sealed with acoustic sealant over the warm side of the insulation before you install drywall. This prevents interior moisture from seeping into the cold framing cavity, avoiding structural rot and mold.

3. Heating Your Insulated Calgary Garage

Once your garage is sealed and insulated, you need a heat source. The two most reliable systems are:

  1. Forced-Air Gas Heaters: Often called "Unit Heaters" (like a Modine Hot Dawg). They mount in the corner of the ceiling and use a gas burner with a fan to circulate hot air quickly. They are cheap to run on natural gas and heat a double garage in under 10 minutes.
  2. Infrared Tube Heaters: Run on natural gas but use infrared rays to heat *objects* (your car, your tools, your body) rather than the air. They are silent, highly energy-efficient, and maintain warmth even when you open the garage door briefly.
